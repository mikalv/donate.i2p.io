About

The Invisible Internet Project began in 2001 as the Invisible IRC Project. Since that time it has continued as a research and development driven project with a focus on providing privacy and security and privacy. It is a self-driven, self-defenced decentralized and peer to peer network.

The Invisible Internet Project (I2P) builds and deploys technology built with security, privacy and consent first. Its network it peer to peer. The network is completely free to use and the project is 100% community funded.

The Invisible Internet Project (I2P) strives towards equity, empowerment and protection. It protects its participants from surveillance and every participant is a equal citizen on its network. The project seeks to provide equal support to development, design, and research. It is committed to movement building with other aligned projects and technologies.

The Invisible Internet Project (I2P) supports the right to information. The project works to constantly improve its circumvention abilities and resistance.

Who Uses The Invisible Internet?

People who are concerned about their privacy and interested in alternatives to the internet make use of the network and its applications to chat, email, torrent and run their own sites and forums. In some circumstances individuals rely on the network to communicate due to safety. Researchers, developers and digital rights defenders are I2P users as well.

How is The Invisible Internet Project Funded and how is that funding used?

The Invisible Internet Project is 100% community funded. In the past the Project has received a grant from DuckDuckGo, and has received support from the OTF Usability Lab in order to support improvements to UX and information architecture.

Donations support I2P maintainers server costs, and other costs associated with keeping the network services up and running. Donations support improving the I2P protocol, and ensuring that the network is accessible and its software is easy to use. The Invisible Internet Project is committed always improving its privacy, security and usability.
