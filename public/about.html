About

The Invisible Internet Project (I2P) began in 2003 as the Invisible IRC Project. Since that time it has continued as a research and development driven project with a focus on providing privacy and protection for communications on its network. It is Open Source and free to connect to and use.

The Invisible Internet provides architectural boundaries - its network is developed with privacy by design. I2P was not designed with profit-driven or intelligence-driven goals. It obfuscates your traffic and puts you in control of your space that you create on its network. You are in control of your discoverability and what you consider private, social or public.

The Invisible Internet Project (I2P) supports the right to access. The project works to constantly improve its circumvention abilities and resistance. Once you are connected to the I2P network, your traffic and communications are protected.

Who Uses I2P?

People who are concerned about their privacy and interested in alternatives to the internet make use of the network and its applications to chat, email, torrent and run their own sites and forums. In some circumstances individuals rely on the network to communicate due to safety issues or censorship. Researchers, developers and digital rights defenders are I2P users as well.
